Celiac disease is a chronic digestive and immune disorder that damages the small intestine

The disease is triggered by eating foods containing gluten

can cause long-lasting digestive problems and keep your body from getting all the nutrients it needs

Symptoms:

Bloating
Chronic diarrhea
Constipation
Gas
Lactose intolerance due to damage to the small intestine
Loose, greasy, bulky, and bad-smelling stools
Nausea or vomiting
Pain in the abdomen
