inflammation of the sheet-like layers that cover the lungs (the pleura). 
The most common symptom of pleurisy is a sharp chest pain when breathing deeply. 
Sometimes the pain is also felt in the shoulder

Causes:

Most cases are the result of a viral infection (such as the flu) or a bacterial infection (such as pneumonia). 

In rarer cases, pleurisy can be caused by conditions such as a blood clot blocking the flow of blood into the lungs (pulmonary embolism) or lung cancer


Symptoms:

Chest pain that worsens when you breathe, 
cough or sneeze. 
Shortness of breath — often from trying to limit breathing in and out.
Cough — only in some cases
