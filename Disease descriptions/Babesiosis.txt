Babesiosis is caused by microscopic parasites that infect red blood cells and are spread by certain ticks

Tickborne transmission is most common in particular regions and seasons in the USA: 

It mainly occurs in parts of the Northeast and upper Midwest and usually peaks during the warm months.


Types of Babesia:

Babesia microti 
Babesia divergens

Symptoms

Flu-like symptoms:

Fever 
Chills
Sweats
Headache
Body aches
Loss of appetite 
Nausea
Fatigue
