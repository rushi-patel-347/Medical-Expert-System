Sinusitis is an infection of the lining of the sinuses near the nose. These infections most often happen after a cold or after an allergy flare-up. There are 4 types: Acute. Symptoms last less than 4 weeks and get better with the right care.

There are several types of sinusitis, including:

1) Acute, which lasts up to 4 weeks
2) Subacute, which lasts 4 to 12 weeks
3) Chronic, which lasts more than 12 weeks and can continue for months or even years
4) Recurrent, with several attacks within a year

Methods for diagnosing chronic sinusitis include:

1) Imaging tests
2) Looking into your sinuses.
3) An allergy test. 
4) Samples from your nasal and sinus discharge (cultures)
